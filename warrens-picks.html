Methodology: How Warren's Picks Work

Warren's Picks uses a 7-component Buffett-style quality scoring system that screens ~900 S&P 500 + S&P 400 companies for characteristics of durable, high-quality businesses.

ROE Quality (20 pts)

Sustained high return on equity — Buffett's primary quality signal. Rewards ROE > 15% with consistency.

Debt Safety (15 pts)

Low debt-to-equity, strong interest coverage. Conservative balance sheet management.

Margin Strength (15 pts)

Operating + gross margin quality. Wide margins indicate pricing power and competitive moats.

FCF Machine (15 pts)

Strong free cash flow yield and FCF-to-net-income conversion. Cash generation is king.

Earnings Consistency (15 pts)

Stable, growing earnings trajectory. Penalizes volatile or declining earnings.

Moat Proxy (10 pts)

Institutional ownership + analyst consensus as proxy for competitive advantages. Smart money signals.

Shareholder Return (10 pts)

Dividends + buybacks. Companies that return capital show management discipline.

Recommendations: STRONG_QUALITY (65+/100) | QUALITY (45+/100). Scores are based on quarterly fundamentals and refresh daily. This is an experimental A/B test against the contrarian approach.
