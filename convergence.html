How convergence works

Rick (the independent assessor) runs 5 blind expert agents with zero scanner knowledge. Each searches the web for opportunities in its domain. A convergence is a ticker that 2+ experts independently flagged — the assessor's highest-conviction output. Each card shows why each expert flagged it, the scanner's own stance, company health, options flow, fresh news, and entry/target/stop.

Streak = consecutive assessor sessions a ticker has been a multi-expert overlap. A 3+ session streak is a much stronger signal than a one-off — the panel keeps independently landing on it.

Assessor + Scanner badge = the scanner also has the ticker as a BUY/WATCHLIST etc. Independent agreement across two systems = strongest conviction.

Data: convergence.json (assessor) · signals/opportunities (scanner) · sec_fundamentals (EDGAR health) · options_snapshots (flow) · catalyst_events + Jina fresh-news (news) · deep_buy entry/target/stop logic. Not financial advice.
